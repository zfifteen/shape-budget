## Persistent Atlas Modes Reveal Stable Ambiguity Folds

The hard branch does not just produce more random candidates. It carries larger persistent families that cluster around specific geometry modes even as compression load rises.

This shows the backbone stays fixed while the hidden coordinate folds into repeatable ambiguity patterns.

What looks like solver failure is actually the atlas exposing discrete directions where observations become non unique.

We would not have predicted that cluster persistence stays high across bank seeds in the high load regime.

This means random banks waste effort on redundant families while an informed bank can target the dominant clusters directly.

Expect that conditioning samples on the top persistent modes will cut compression load by at least twenty percent without losing backbone recovery.

The threshold appears when compression exceeds roughly twelve candidates per backbone while persistence stays above point nine.
